Tourism Income and Accommodation Analysis

OverviewThis project conducts an in-depth analysis of the relationship between the total income of tourist accommodations and the average number of conventional dwellings across various municipalities and years. The aim is to discover patterns and correlations that can provide valuable insights for local government bodies and tourism boards.

Methodology

MethodologyThe analysis pipeline involves the use of several data visualization techniques and machine learning algorithms, such as scatter plots, t-SNE visualization, KMeans Clustering, correlation matrix, and time series plots.

  • Scatter plots
  • t-SNE Visualization
  • KMeans Clustering
  • Correlation Matrix
  • Time Series and Scatter plots

  1. Scatter Plots

    Scatter plots are used to visualize the relationship between the total income of tourist accommodations and the average number of conventional dwellings, with data points being color-coded by year.

  2. t-SNE Visualization

    t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t-SNE) is a machine learning algorithm used for visualizing high-dimensional data. In this project, t-SNE is employed to reduce the dimensionality of the data and display it in a 2D scatter plot.

  3. KMeans Clustering

    The KMeans algorithm, an unsupervised machine learning method, is used to categorize municipalities into three distinct clusters based on their numerical features.

  4. Correlation Matrix

    A correlation matrix is used to visualize the degree to which different features of the dataset correlate with each other. A heatmap was employed to visually represent this correlation matrix.

  5. Time Series and Scatter plots

    These plots are used to display how the total income of tourist accommodations changes over time by municipality, and the relationship between total income of tourist accommodations and guests per 100 by municipality.

Results and Deployment

This project analyzes the impact of the tourism industry by studying the accommodation sector in the Lisbon metropolitan area. Data from Pordata was used to examine indicators such as the number of bedrooms, capacity, guests, income, and the correlation with foreign guests and conventional dwellings. The analysis revealed an overall growth in the number of bedrooms, capacity, guests, nights spent, and income. There was also a positive correlation among these indicators. Additionally, the proportion of foreign guests varied across municipalities, and there was a negative correlation between the proportion of foreign guests and the average number of conventional dwellings.
